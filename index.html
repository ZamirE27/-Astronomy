ASTRONOMY

ABOUT SPACE

Astronomy is the natural science that studies everything related to the celestial bodies that exist in the universe, as well as the phenomena that occur between them or affect them. Astronomy covers everything from the study of stars, planets, satellites, asteroids, comets, and meteoroids, to that of nebulae, galaxies, dark matter, black holes, and cosmic radiation. Astronomy is also concerned with the origin, evolution, and ultimate fate of the universe as a whole through cosmology.

It analyzes the information that comes from celestial bodies in the form of electromagnetic radiation or other signals. Optical and infrared telescopes on Earth or in orbit are used, as well as radio telescopes to capture radio waves, space telescopes to detect X-rays or gamma rays, interferometers to combine signals from multiple telescopes and increase their resolution, or gravitational wave detectors to measure disturbances in space-time caused by accelerated massive objects.
